BingoBongoTesting

Introduction

BingoBongo testing is a simple but efficient, explorative test methodology to find issues and have fun.

Preparation

BingoBongo works best if you focus on an specific part or feature of your application.

Simply

  • adapt this template to your test case
  • invite the testers; 4-8 persons
  • depending on the complexity of the feature to be tested the meeting should take 1h-1.5h
  • make sure everybody has access to the application
  • make sure everybody is able to share the screen

The Meeting

Every BingoBongo meetings strictly follows the same rules.

  • Initial presentation done by the moderator
    • The Goal
    • The Rules
    • Introduction / Demo of the feature that will be tested (either by the moderator, a developer or requirements expert)
    • Known Issues & Test focus
  • The Game
    • Everybody tests on his own
    • If you find something - shout 'BingoBongo'
    • Everyone stops working; the Finder shares his/her screen and presents the finding
    • If everyone agrees, the Finder has to create an issue in the bugtracker. After the issue is created, the finder gets a point
    • Repeat...
  • The Winner is...
    • Have a final look at the results and congratulation

Why it works

  • Competition makes the testers focus on the work during the limited time
  • Experience shows, that many more issues are detected during a BingoBongo session compared to conventional testing
  • Sharing where an issue was found leads the testers to explore workflows they have not considered before
  • To get an advantage, testers try out alternatives besides main workflows
  • The session should be competitive, but fair: Tester should exchange working examples of test data
  • We found that even end users like to take part in BingoBongo sessions

History

The first BingoBongo test session was held at 2019-10-10 between 14:15-15:30 somewhere in Leverkusen/Germany. There was only a short team attending to this session - in fact Wiebke Höhn, Maik Zielinsky, Frank Augustin and me did this (for a screenshot fof the invitation for this session see below).
This first session was not named BingoBongo; the name was used for all following sessions.
